pizza

ingrediants

  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup warm water (105°F–110°F or 40°C–43°C)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for the bowl

  • for the sause

  • ½ cup crushed canned tomatoes or tomato puree
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • ¼ tsp dried oregano
  • Pinch of salt and black pepper

    • For The Toppings

  • 4-6 oz shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Desired toppings: e.g., pepperoni, sliced bell peppers, onions, mushrooms
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Activate the yeast: In a large bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes foamy. If it doesn't foam, the yeast is likely expired, and you should start over with a fresh packet
  2. Combine ingredients: Add the flour, salt, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the yeast mixture. Mix with a wooden spoon or your hands until a shaggy dough forms
  3. Knead the dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Knead for about 5-7 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  4. Let it rise: Grease a clean large bowl with olive oil and place the dough ball inside, turning to coat it. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Place it in a warm spot and let it rise until it doubles in size, which typically takes 1 to 2 hours
  5. Punch down: Once doubled, gently punch the dough down to release the air bubbles
  6. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, mix the crushed tomatoes, minced garlic, olive oil, oregano, salt, and pepper. This is a no-cook sauce; simply mix and spread.
  7. Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to its highest possible temperature (ideally 450°F to 500°F or 230°C to 260°C) for at least 30 minutes. If you have a pizza stone or cast-iron skillet, place it in the oven while preheating for a crispier crust.
  8. Shape the dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll or stretch the dough ball into a round shape, about 10-12 inches in diameter. Leave the edges slightly thicker for the crust.
  9. Assemble the pizza: Transfer the stretched dough to a pizza peel or a baking sheet dusted with cornmeal or flour. Spread a thin layer of sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a border for the crust. Sprinkle with mozzarella cheese and add your desired toppings.
  10. Bake: Slide the pizza onto the preheated stone or baking sheet in the oven. Bake for 8-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  11. Serve: Remove the pizza from the oven, garnish with fresh basil if using, slice, and serve immediately.
